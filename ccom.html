Machine Learning Research Intern

Center for Coastal and Ocean Mapping (CCOM), University of New Hampshire — Durham

About CCOM

The Center for Coastal and Ocean Mapping (CCOM) is a leading oceanographic research institute focused on marine mapping, underwater acoustics, and coastal systems.

Project Overview

Worked with a graduate researcher studying ocean habitats using long-term underwater acoustic data. Hydrophones were deployed for 6 months to collect continuous ocean sound recordings for ecological analysis.

One major dataset was partially corrupted due to improper placement near marine vegetation, which introduced persistent mechanical noise throughout the recording period. Re-collecting the data would have required an additional 6-month deployment.

My Contribution

Led a machine-learning–driven signal denoising project to recover usable data from the corrupted recordings.

Key Contributions

  • Led the project end-to-end: research, scoping, sprint planning, implementation, and validation
  • Designed and implemented supervised ML + signal processing methods in MATLAB to separate environmental noise from biological signals
  • Processed 20+ GB of underwater acoustic data (10M+ samples)
  • Recovered 30–40% of otherwise unusable data, eliminating the need for re-sampling
  • Saved ~6 months (~480 hours) of research time and accelerated ongoing studies
  • Developed reusable MATLAB scripts adopted by 5+ researchers
  • Work presented at Acoustics 2023 (Sydney, Australia)
